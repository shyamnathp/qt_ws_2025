The Path Forward 🛣️

  • Option 1: Rewrite Qt in new languages
    • ❌ Massive effort
    • ❌ Loss of maturity
    • ❌ Fragmentation risk
  • Option 2: Create language bindings for Qt
    • ✅ Preserve Qt's maturity
    • ✅ Incremental adoption
    • ✅ Proven approach (PySide6)
  • Option 3: Unified frontend, multiple backends
    • QML as the universal frontend
    • Simplified backend integration
    • Lower entry barrier

  • Shiboken Wizard

Comparing Binding Tools ⚔️

  • Compare Shiboken with other binding generators:
    • pybind11: Lightweight, header-only library for C++ bindings, but lacks Qt-specific features.
    • Boost.Python: Comprehensive but outdated and heavy; less commonly used today.
    • nanobind: Modern alternative to pybind11, optimized for performance but not Qt-focused.
    • SIP: Used by PyQt, tightly integrated but less flexible than Shiboken for large frameworks like Qt.
  • Highlight Shiboken's unique advantages for Qt:
    • Specifically designed for Qt's complex APIs.
    • Non-intrusive: No need to modify Qt's source code.
    • Seamless integration with PySide6 and QML.
- Discuss the strengths and weaknesses of other binding tools like pybind11, Boost.Python, nanobind, and SIP. - Emphasize why Shiboken is the best choice for Qt developers.

Beyond Python 🌟
